Attica, Athens

Greece

Greece, a country steeped in ancient history and cultural significance, is home to Attica, a region renowned for its historical richness and captivating landscapes. At its heart lies Athens, the capital city, revered as the cradle of Western civilization.

Acropolis of Athens

The Acropolis is the most iconic site in Athens, home to the Parthenon, the Erechtheion, and the Temple of Athena Nike. It sits atop a hill overlooking the city, offering breathtaking views of Athens and its surrounding mountains.

Temple of Poseidon at Cape Sounion

Located at the southernmost tip of the Attica peninsula, the Temple of Poseidon offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea.

Plaka and Anafiotika

Plaka is the historic neighborhood at the foot of the Acropolis, with narrow, winding streets, neoclassical buildings, and vibrant tavernas.

Mount Lycabettus

This hill is the highest point in Athens and offers panoramic views of the city and the sea. You can either hike to the top or take a funicular.

Lake Vouliagmeni

Located just outside of Athens, Lake Vouliagmeni is a natural thermal lake surrounded by dramatic cliffs. The mineral-rich waters are known for their therapeutic properties, making it a popular spot for relaxation and swimming./p>
