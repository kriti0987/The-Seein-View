Manarola Cinque,Terre

Northern Italy

Manarola is one of the five charming villages nestled along the rugged coastline of the Cinque Terre in Northern Italy. With its colorful houses perched on steep cliffs overlooking the Ligurian Sea, Manarola enchants visitors with its picturesque beauty and relaxed atmosphere.

Manarola Harbor

The small harbor is the heart of Manarola, with colorful boats, steep cliffs, and crystal-clear waters. It’s a great place to swim, relax by the sea, or take in the picturesque view of the village.

Via dell’Amore (The Path of Love)

This romantic coastal walkway connects Manarola with the nearby village of Riomaggiore.

Church of San Lorenzo

Dating back to 1338, this beautiful Gothic-style church is dedicated to Saint Lawrence and is one of Manarola’s historic landmarks.

Punta Bonfiglio

This scenic lookout point offers one of the best views of Manarola, with its iconic colorful houses clinging to the cliffs.

Cantina Sociale Manarola

Manarola is famous for its local wine, particularly Sciacchetrà, a sweet dessert wine. The Cantina Sociale is a local winery where you can taste and buy wines produced in the region's terraced vineyards.
